About

I am finishing my PhD in Genetics and Genomic Sciences at the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai (defending October 2026). My research uncovers novel genotype-phenotype associations through large-scale analysis of tandem repeat expansions across the UK Biobank and All of Us in over 350,000 genomes, integrating WGS, methylation, RNA-seq, and long-read data to characterize functional impact.

I am looking for industry R&D roles where statistical genomics expertise can drive target discovery and human-genetics-driven therapeutic development.

Selected Research

2020 — Present  ·  Sharp Lab, Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai

PhD Candidate

  • Discovered 19 novel TR expansion loci associated with neurodegenerative disease by meta-analyzing >350,000 samples across UK Biobank and All of Us using REGENIE with approximate Firth logistic regression.
  • Genotyped >77,000 TRs and identified expansions in >100,000 All of Us samples, accounting for batch effects and cohort heterogeneity.
  • Integrated WGS SNP, methylation array, bulk RNA-seq, and long-read sequencing across AMP PD, GTEx, TOPMed, ADSP, and AoU to characterize the functional and clinical impact of TR expansions.
  • Built containerized, reproducible cloud pipelines (Cavatica/AWS) to scale TR genotyping and de novo expansion calling for thousands of WGS trios in pediatric congenital disorders.
  • Screened LDLR promoter hypermethylation in 308 mutation-negative familial hypercholesterolemia patients via Combined Bisulfite Restriction Analysis.

Splice Site Prediction with Deep Learning

PyTorch CNN BiLSTM Genomics

Compared six model architectures (Random Forest, CNN, uni- and bidirectional LSTMs, two CNN/BiLSTM hybrids) for donor and acceptor splice site classification on the HS3D dataset. Extracted first-layer CNN filter weights to surface candidate motifs; downstream MEME/XSTREME analysis recovered known splice site signals. Built for the BDS 3002 Machine Learning for Biomedical Data Science final project at ISMMS.

Co-authors: Carina Seah and O'Jay Stewart

Mammography Patch Classification with Ensemble Learning

PyTorch DenseNet121 Transfer Learning Ensemble Methods

Developed a 10-model soft-voting ensemble for classifying mammography image patches into five categories (benign/malignant calcifications and masses, background). Used two-stage transfer learning (freeze backbone → fine-tune full network) and WeightedRandomSampler to handle severe class imbalance. Built during the BDS 3002 Machine Learning for Biomedical Data Science Hackathon at ISMMS.

Co-authors: Carina Seah and O'Jay Stewart
